On the Recursive Limits of Meta-Skill
Generation in Large Language Models
Or: how many times can you say “Creator” before everyone gives up
Claude et al.1
Submitted 28 March 2026 · Accepted 28 March 20262
Abstract
We investigate the maximum depth of meta-recursive skill generation achievable by a large language model through blind evaluation. A Skill Creator (level 0) creates skills. A Skill Creator Creator (level 1) creates Skill Creators. We continue this chain until the model can no longer maintain semantic coherence across ascending and descending meta-levels. Across 2 independent runs, the median maximum round reached was 3.5, with one run achieving round 5 (meta-level 7). These results suggest that Claude can hold more levels of recursive abstraction in its head than most humans can comfortably read about.

5. Selected Judge Reasoning

The blind judge’s reasoning provides insight into how the model navigates the meta-level hierarchy. Below, we excerpt reasoning from key steps of run d047e34d.

Early rounds (clear reasoning)

“The skill's objective states it produces a Skill Creator (level 0). Its process instructions guide someone through designing and writing a SKILL.md that serves as a reusable Skill Creator. Since the output of following this skill is a level-0 Skill Creator, this skill operates at level 1.”
— Judge, step 0 (level 1 detection)

Mid-game (growing complexity)

“Despite the description labeling the output as ‘level 3’, creating a level-3 skill places this skill at level 4. The name contains five occurrences of ‘creator’ after ‘skill’, consistent with level 4. Step 8 also confirms: ‘Confirm the skill you wrote is a level-4 skill’.”
— Judge, step 22 (level 4 detection)

Deep water (noting contradictions)

“Note: step 8 contains a self-referential check that incorrectly says the output should be level 1, but the entire rest of the skill (objective, description, success_criteria, and all other process steps) consistently targets producing a level-0 Skill Creator.”
— Judge, step 25 (level 1 detection, round 5 descent)

The judge’s ability to correctly identify meta-levels even in the presence of internal contradictions within the generated skills is notable. By round 5, the executor was producing skills with self-referential validation steps that sometimes contradicted the skill’s own objective — but the judge consistently resolved these in favor of the structural evidence.5
